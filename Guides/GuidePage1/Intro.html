What are Cryptocurrencies?

Cryptocurrency is a tokenized asset that is a digital representation of value, money in a digital space. Cryptocurrencies reside mainly for investments similar to stock market trading. It can also be used to make transactions around in the real world. Currently it is moving towards physical value, where one can use cryptocurrency to buy material value like Tesla.

Types of Cryptocurrencies

There are many different types of Cryptocurrencies. They all come in the form of tokens which are tied to the specific blockchain technology that they have been coded with.

Some of the more commonly recognised Cryptocurrencies : Bitcoin, Litecoin, Dogecoin, Ethereum, USDC (Stable coin), and Many More!

The following are the different tokens categorised accordingly to their purpose:

Types of Tokens:
# Cryptocurrency Type Purpose
1 Currency Tokens Used to trade unpriceable and high value items, price of gold
2 Exchange Tokens Used for exchanging tokens
3 Payment tokens Used for purchasing goods and services mainly in the real world.
4 NFTs Used to own a licensing of a digital artwork / animation. Unique & non-fungible. Cryptos are fungible.
5 DeFi tokens (Decentralised Finance Tokens) Popular amongst traders. Access to bank-like services such as loans, lending & insurance

Cryptocurrency Basics

# Cryptocurrency Terms Description
1 Crypto value in $ Allows for easier comparisons between different coins
2 Crypto Supply Limited supply: Volatile pricing based on trading demand. Unlimited supply: Lesser value in the long term.
3 Trading volume Amount of trading being done on the cryptocurrency
4 Market capitalization Total amount of coins * current price = current market cap.
5 Crypto holders Information of who holds the coins (investments). Projects & companies to raise money
6 Burning Tokens Takes coins out of circulation. lesser coins = price appreciation
7 Crypto ICOs (initial coin offerings) and IDOs (initial dex offerings) Project to issue cryptocurrencies to earn $$ to build their projects.

  • Currency pair trading
    • What is a Cryptocurrency Pair?

    When trading crypto, you must trade in pairs. When you buy something, you have to sell something in return. This is where stable coins come into play.

    Things to note:

  • Not every currency pair is supported
  • Stable coins and BTC will trade against most cryptos

    • What are stable coins?

    Stable coins are known as fiat currencies in the crypto space. These coins are crypto’s versions of real assets. Some of these coins are backed by “real world” currencies or assets, where prices are pegged to these assets. These coins are very important to help with liquidity within cryptocurrencies. These coins are used as a “gateway” in and out of the cryptocurrency market, used as a gateway to buy or exit out.

    Examples of Stable Coins (these coins are all worth $1 USD)

  • USD Coin (USDC)
  • Tether (USDT)
  • Binance USD (BUSD)
    • Base vs Quote Currency

    Base currency 🡪 Buying currency

    Quote currency 🡪 Selling currency

    Quote currency tells you how much is needed to buy 1 unit of base currency

    Base currency is on the left, Quote currency is on the right

    Examples:

  • BTC / USDT 50,000
  • BTC / USDT +5% (percentage move of a day)
