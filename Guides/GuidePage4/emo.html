Here's why controlling emotions is important when trading crypto

How do our emotions get involved?

Flight or fight responses: These are common emotions that stocks / cryptocurrency traders experience daily.
When the market is up, that adrenaline rush and excitement indirectly creates fear of missing out(FOMO) to users who hav not invested in it. When prices fall due to an economic crash or a global pandemic, it is both hard for people to enter the market and people may start panic selling as a way to minimise losses.
When emotions go into self-defence mode, your brain naturally takes immediate actions without putting much thought into it. This is why a lot of people massively buy at the top and sell at the bottom because emotions are getting the better of them.
The issue lies within media presence about cryptocurrency trading. Majority of reported stories show the overtly positive exceptions: “Person has earned US$2 million in like 4 months”, giving a false impression and fallacy of creating the “get rich quick” mindset with unrealistic expectations.
This compounds with the existence of “gurus” and “experts” coming in abundance within social media, posting technical analysis and know-hows, telling people which is the next coin that is “going to the moon”. It is very hard to know who's well qualified, and who is just posting public charts and giving bad trading advice. There is also the possibility of them looking to earn quick bucks by promoting their alt coins. All these factors drastically affect the way people trade and end up in huge negatives.
When people do a losing trade, they adopt the mindset of a gambler, trying to win back their losses. People call this suicide trading. Whatever is lost needs to be won back multiple times to break-even, leading you to trade bigger sums and take riskier approaches, eventually losing it all.

What is Trading Psychology?

Trading Psychology is about controlling your emotions. Knowing where the market sentiment is currently at as well as your individual emotions and sentiments is important.
Therefore you need to come up with a trading plan. A trading plan consists of a strategy with a set of rules that you follow every time you place a trade. This strategy should be based on a set of rules to make it easy to follow, which allows for an easier way to control your emotions better and to help make more consistent and better decisions in the long run.
Trading Psychology is not what you do while you are in a trade. It is what you do before you pressed the “BUY” button on the trading platform.

Methods to practising Trading Psychology

1) Adopting trading strategies from well qualified traders

Building up a trading plan comes with experience. The more experience you gain from trading, the more robust your set of rules will be to follow to help with emotions.
Therefore, start out by adopting a trading plan from a well-known trader.
Always research on markets and prominent people.
Make an accurate judgements before following their proposed trading plan.

2) Backtest your strategy countless times

Take your trading plan and test it with the historical charts of the market. Backtesting gives confidence to your strategy and naturally disciplines your emotions to not determine decisions.
Results from one single trade are unpredictable. Therefore, backtest your strategy with small costs repeatedly.
Example: Starting out with 100 trades of 10 cents, then increase slowly to 1000, 10000 and so on.
These large sample size tests will indicate whether your strategy is making you money. Through backtests, you gain more experience with trading. With this experience, you can modify your existing trading plan or come up with a new trading plan that suits you best to give you the highest probability for profits.

3) Have a Solid Risk Management Plan

Ask yourself this question: “How much am I comfortable with losing on this trade?”
Losses are inevitable from time to time. Get yourself mentally prepared and always assume that whatever money you are willing to trade with will be lost.
Approach each trade with caution and risk assessment, so you will not have yourself going into the “fear mode”, rest is self-explanatory.
