Why Cryptocurrency?

Big companies prioritise selling other peoples’ data like purchasing habits and potentially sensitive identity data to other companies.

Therefore, cryptocurrency is a “decentralised” environment that uses blockchain technology. In blockchain, decentralisation refers to the transfer of control and decision-making from a centralised entity (individual, organisation, or group thereof) to a distributed network. Traders believe this helps to alleviate downsides of using a primarily peer-to-peer system (banks) that relies heavily on other entities outside of their own control.

Cryptocurrency has a low barrier of entry to trading with low fee costs (you can even start off with just $1) and the trading environment runs 24/7. This allows transparency between parties with real-time visibility of the data and documentation of both trading & transactions.
